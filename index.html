T2DM Equity-Efficiency Decision Aid Tool

This interactive tool helps researchers and policymakers evaluate how changes in risk, efficacy, and monthly costs can shape preferences for T2DM treatments across different equity-efficiency scenarios.

Introduction

This tool evaluates how patients choose among T2DM treatment plans under three experimental conditions:

  1. Experiment 1 (Self-Focused Outcomes): Focus solely on personal risk, efficacy, and cost.
  2. Experiment 2 (Equal Health Outcomes): Health outcomes are shared equally between the individual and others with poorer health.
  3. Experiment 3 (Unequal Outcomes): Disparate outcomes for self and others, allowing more complex equity trade-offs.

By adjusting risk, efficacy, and monthly out-of-pocket cost, you can see how these attributes affect plan uptake probability, willingness to pay (WTP), and overall cost-benefit of adopting certain T2DM treatments.

Instructions

  1. Attributes: Check "Attributes" for definitions. Risk references 0% baseline, Efficacy references 10% baseline.
  2. Inputs: In the "Inputs" tab, select an experiment and configure risk, efficacy, and cost. If choosing Experiment 3, also provide "others" attributes.
  3. Predicted Uptake: Click “Show Probability Plot.” The result is displayed in the "Predicted Health Plan Uptake" tab.
  4. WTP Results: Explore bar charts for WTP in "WTP Results." Negative WTP indicates disutility; positive WTP indicates a willingness to pay for improvements.
  5. Scenarios: Save scenarios for comparison or exporting to PDF in the "Scenarios" tab.
  6. Costs & Benefits: Review cost breakdown and QALY-based net benefit in the "Costs & Benefits" tab.

Key Attributes

Efficacy

Chance of achieving target A1C in 6 months. (10% baseline, 50%, 90%).

Efficacy

Higher efficacy suggests stronger T2DM control outcomes.

Risk

Probability of side effects above 0% baseline (e.g., 8%, 16%, 30%).

Risk

Greater risk percentages require compensation to offset disutility.

Cost

Monthly out-of-pocket cost from $0 to $1000 for T2DM coverage.

Monthly Out-of-Pocket Cost

Higher cost tends to reduce likelihood of choosing the plan.

We list cost components relevant to T2DM management (fixed vs. variable) and multiply the uptake-based fraction for variable costs. Benefits are quantified as QALYs gained (low, moderate, or high) and monetized at $50,000 per QALY. Net benefit is monetized benefits minus total costs.
